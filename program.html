Graduate Program

M.S. Instructional Design & Technology

A practitioner-focused graduate program at Georgia State University preparing the next generation of learning design professionals.

Program Overview

Designed for Real-World Impact

The M.S. in Instructional Design & Technology at Georgia State University is a rigorous, practice-oriented graduate program that prepares students to design, develop, and evaluate effective learning solutions across corporate, government, healthcare, and educational contexts.

Under Dr. Law's coordination, the program has deepened its emphasis on corporate and enterprise learning — connecting curriculum directly to the competencies most sought after by leading organizations. Students graduate as performance consultants, not just course designers.

The program is built on the premise that instructional design is a discipline of evidence and judgment. Students learn foundational learning theory, needs analysis, project management, eLearning development tools, and program evaluation — always in service of measurable performance outcomes.

Student Outcomes

What Graduates Can Do

Conduct systematic needs assessments and performance gap analyses in complex organizational settings
Design and develop evidence-based instructional solutions aligned with organizational performance goals
Develop eLearning content using industry-standard tools including Articulate 360, Adobe Captivate, and LMS platforms
Apply evaluation frameworks (Kirkpatrick, Phillips ROI) to measure learning effectiveness and business impact
Consult with business stakeholders, manage projects, and communicate design rationale to non-specialist audiences
Lead and contribute to enterprise learning strategy as L&D managers, instructional designers, and performance consultants
Curriculum

Sample Coursework

Core courses reflect the intersection of theory, technology, and professional practice.

IDT 7410
Foundations of Instructional Design

Survey of learning theories, instructional design models, and the systems approach to designing effective learning.

IDT 7420
Needs Assessment & Performance Analysis

Methods for identifying performance gaps, root cause analysis, and selecting appropriate interventions.

IDT 7430
eLearning Design & Development

Hands-on development using Articulate 360 and other industry tools; UX principles for digital learning environments.

IDT 7440
Learning Management Systems

Administration, configuration, and analytics for enterprise LMS platforms; SCORM, xAPI, and integration standards.

IDT 7450
Evaluation of Learning & Performance

Kirkpatrick Model, Phillips ROI, and mixed-methods evaluation design for corporate and educational contexts.

IDT 7460
Corporate Learning Strategy

Strategic alignment of learning initiatives with organizational goals; L&D governance, portfolio management, and measurement.

IDT 7470
Emerging Technologies in Learning

AI, AR/VR, microlearning, and adaptive systems — evaluating and applying emerging technologies in organizational contexts.

IDT 7490
Capstone: Applied Instructional Design

End-to-end design project with a real organizational client — from needs analysis through evaluation planning.

IDT 7480
Research Methods in IDT

Quantitative and qualitative research methods; literature review and evidence-based practice in learning design.

* Course numbers and titles are representative. Current course offerings subject to change. Visit the GSU Graduate Catalog for official program requirements.
