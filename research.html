Research Focus

Areas of Scholarly Interest

Dr. Law's research sits at the boundary of academic scholarship and organizational practice — investigating questions that matter both to the field of instructional design and to the practitioners who apply it.

Corporate Learning Effectiveness

Measuring the transfer and organizational impact of enterprise learning programs across large, complex organizations.

Performance Consulting Practice

How instructional designers function as performance consultants and the competencies required to influence business outcomes.

Technology-Enhanced Learning

The role of emerging technologies — including AI, adaptive learning, and immersive media — in corporate and higher education contexts.

IDT Program Outcomes

Practitioner preparation in graduate IDT programs: competency development, industry alignment, and career readiness.

Evaluation & ROI

Application of the Phillips ROI Methodology and Kirkpatrick Model in organizational settings; barriers to rigorous evaluation practice.

L&D Strategy & Governance

How learning functions are structured, governed, and strategically positioned in Fortune 500 organizations.
